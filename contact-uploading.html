Uploading & Non-Users

Snafic provides a structured access system that distinguishes between registered users and non-users to maintain platform quality, security, and content integrity. Non-users are allowed to explore limited features of the platform, giving them an opportunity to understand the system before creating an account.

This approach helps new visitors experience the interface, view public content, and learn about the platform's capabilities without immediately requiring registration. It creates a smooth onboarding experience that encourages users to join naturally after exploring the platform.

Uploading content is a privileged feature reserved for registered and verified users. This ensures that all shared content meets community guidelines, quality standards, and safety requirements. By restricting uploads, Snafic minimizes spam, misuse, and harmful content while maintaining a trustworthy environment.

Users who wish to upload content must complete a registration process that may include basic identity verification and account setup. This helps maintain accountability and ensures that all content contributors are part of the trusted community.

Non-users are still considered an important part of the ecosystem. They represent potential future users and are given a preview experience designed to showcase the value of the platform. This strategy helps improve conversion rates while maintaining security and stability.

Snafic is also developing smart onboarding systems that guide non-users through the platform step-by-step, helping them understand features and benefits before signing up. These systems are designed to make the transition from visitor to user smooth and engaging.

In conclusion, the Uploading & Non-Users system is designed to balance openness with security, allowing exploration while maintaining strict control over content creation.
